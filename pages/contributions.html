From the family I feel with the housemates - and the five years we spent together - to the boys at 83B and my dawg Vinny. These are the mans.

Hahahaha one thing about Sammy is that he would always laugh at me when I was in pain, but it was in a good way. The one time, we were in our fourth year. At this point I was coaching, but I would come in and play in practices if we needed another player. It was Christmas break and I had done that earlier in the day and hurt my back pretty bad. We were the only two at our student house because Ross was still in Caledon and the Graham's had gone home for the evening. Usually when I would hurt my back it would tighten up that day and then slowly get better, so later in the day I hurt it would be the most pain I would be in. He was laughing the entire time, which made me laugh too. Although it isn't ecaxtly fun to get laughed at while you're in pain, it did make me laugh at myself - something that helps keep a somewhat positive attitude at the very least.

Jesse thought it would be worthwhile to give a short guide to my vocabulary:

I was fortunate enough to live with Russell for 4 years during my time at Queen’s university. In that time however, I was able to decipher the many strange noises that accompanied Russell’s speech. We often liked to poke fun at the various phrases and the onomatopoeia that Russell would weave into normal, Canadian, human speech. Below you will find the various noises, followed by their time of use, a short definition of the term, and an example in everyday use. Enjoy!

Merp (September 2014 – April 2019) – One of the more common Russell phrases “merp” is often used to signal indifference to a given subject.

Example:

Jesse: “Hey Russ, want to go to the movies”

Russ: *contemplates his bank account of $12.95 “merp… sure”

Styll (September 2014 – April 2019) – Again this isn’t original but when Russell uses the term it is distinctive. The Queen’s men’s basketball team recruits a lot of its players from the Toronto area and thus it brings a whole subsection of the English language to the locker room. This language has been coined “Toronto mans slang.” To the unqualified ear this can sound like an entirely different language. However, like many of his teammates outside the GTA, Russell began adopting phrases from the slang language and one of the most noteworthy was the term styll. Styll is a difficult word to define as it can mean to agree with someone or it can signify a sentence has ended.

Example:

Russell: “That’s crazy styll.”

No Food…No water…Help

A short story

By: Tanner Graham & Russell Winters

It was a regular Sunday afternoon for me. I had a few (too many) drinks Saturday night so I took refuge at my parents’ house where my mom could make me waffles and I could nap in the peace and quiet of my parents’ house. However, soon after breakfast I received a disturbing voice message out of the blue from Russell Winters that made this day quite memorable (This wasn’t a voicemail or a phone call, this was one of those weird voice messages you can send on iPhones). At this point in time Russell was basically my third brother. He would come over to our house all the time and chill with my family most weekends. Anyway, this is a direct quote of what Russell said in the voice message in the most feeble and desperate tone I’ve ever heard him speak: “no food…no water…haaalp”. I had just been drinking with Russell the night before so I was very empathetic to this message, I think anyone who has ever got drunk has felt how Russell did this morning. However, most people don’t express these emotions over an umprompted voice message to their friends. After hearing this message I’m thinking I’m sure he’s fine he’ll just ask if he can come to my parent’s place soon to chill as that happened most weekends. But 5 minutes passed and there were no further messages from Russell. Now I’m like ok he might actually need food, water and help. so, I hop in my car and drive to the residence building (Vic hall) that Russell and I both lived in at the time. When I get there, I give Russ a call and simply say “I’m outside Vic hall waiting for you” to which he replies something like “what? Why?” and now I’ll let Russell explain the rest of this story.

My side of the story is very simple:

For some context, this was over Christmas break during my first year and the story takes place on New Years. I had flown back from New York the week before and arrived to my residence building to find no hot water in the washrooms, meaning I couldn't take the hot shower I'd been wanting for hours, there was nowhere to order food from, becasue I had gotten in very late on December 26th, and I was completely alone in Vic Hall. With that in mind, late at night on December 26th, I called Tanner and basically said I had no food, no hot water so I couldn't shower, and would greatly appreciate it if I could stay at his parents' place with them all. He promptly picked me up and that was that.

Move forward now to New Years Day, I was hanging out with Ross and some other friends in residence when I told this story to them - we were all talking about when we got back to campus afte the break. i lifted my phone to my ear to act like I was calling the Grahams and half-heartedly said food, water, help, as to quickly move along with the story. Little did I know, my phone tapped the record message button, because my phone was open and I was texting Tanner at the time. Sure enough, 15 minutes later Tanner gives me a call that he's outside waiting for me. At this point, I'm in a t-shirt and sweats. It's -15 outside at least, and I have absolutely no clue why Tanner is expecting me to be ready to go to his place and makes no mention of Ross, who was sitting there with me. Regardless, we both got ready and went back to the Grahams and soon learned of the message I had sent. It really was a pathetic message.
