Why I'm Making this Page

Overview

This page, itself, is a project. I wanted to see how creative I could get with the HTML and CSS I've learned over the summer. I thought this was a great opportunity to do as much as I could in order to see how much I really know and attempt to stretch the limits of what I know and what I can produce at this point in time.

The assignment is pretty basic: create a 3-page website. Within that, the content is ours to create and the structure needed to follow a few simple guidelines. I wanted to make something personal - not for the sake of sharing tons of information abotu myself, but because it's the easiest content to create.

Why this content?

The content I chose for this really fell in line with having personal content that is easy to create. Since I've just left Queen's, I thought the past 5 years I spent there would provide lots of easy content to generate, as well as a cool ability to allow my friends to be involved in what I'm doing here.

The next two pages are about my time at Queen's and some of the people that made my time at Queen's as great as it was. The first page is an overview of each year I spent there. I just ahve a simple blog post for each year, along with a small gallery of images that I went back and found from those years. I wanted to do that because I thought it would be fun to look through all those memories, but also because I find self-reflection an important part of life. Essentially, I thought that page was a good place to reflect on each year at Queen's - given some distance from Kingston.

Overwiew of my time at Queen's

I spent five years at Queen's. Not exactly because of an inability to complete a four year degree in four years, but because Canadian University Athletics allow for five years of eligibility, so I went into school planning to use all of my eligibility.

That point brings me to the reason I was drawn to Queen's, and what I valued when choosing Queen's: the basketball team. It wasn't the on-court basketball stuff that really drew me in, it was the family I saw between the players when I was on my visit. Seeing that and knowing it was exactly what I wanted to be a part of when I was at school.

Luckily for me, that's what I got. My recruiting class was five players (technically six, but I don't need to get into that) and we all lived together each year after residence in our first year. We didn't all play the full five years - only two of the boys made it - but that was the core and that was the base for the family I felt like we had at QMBB.

That core - Ross, Sammy, Tanner and Jesse - make up four of the seven guys I include on the last page of the website. I wanted to make a space for them to cotnribute to this, so I asked them along with Vinny, Henry and Jake to send me a paragraph or some sort of post, if they wanted to, so I could send them the final result with all their posts to see. My thought was that if they choose not to send me anything I would just add in a little blurb about each of them, since they all had a significant and positivie impact on my time at Queen's.

The End.
